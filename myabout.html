Wengen, Switzerland

Wengen is beautiful. The small town in Switzerland is only accessible by train. This run started in the center of town, up a steep switchback trail, and as you summit you find yourself running at the base of the Eiger.

Engelberg, Switzerland

If you had asked me ten years ago if I ever thought of going to Switzerland to run, I probably would have laughed hysterically. Engelberg changed that. This was the first race I ran overseas. This trail starts with nearly all of the vertical climb in the first mile, they nickname this part of the trail "The Gutter" because of how much water runs down it when it rains. Once on top, you run through pastures filled with Swiss cows, the views of blue mountain lakes reflecting the massive mountains behind you.

Chamonix, France

Chamonix is my absolute favorite place in the world. I think of it as the Mecca of trail running. Nestled in a narrow valley, you are completely surrounded by the Alps. This makes it so whenever you want to go on a run, you need to climb about 3,000 feet just to get to the trailhead. You need to prepare for every run here to be a long run, but the awe of the views completely shadow any negative feelings you may have.

Big Cottonwood Canyon, Utah

This was my first ever full marathon. This run is unique because it is mostly downhill, which makes for fast mile times but painful knees.

Alta, Utah

Alta is where I fell in love with running. Right in my backyard, my experience skiing here gave me sense of familiarity when I decided to start running up mountains. This continues to be one of my favorite places to run.

Brighton, Utah

Running at Brighton is crazy. You start on an immediate and relentless uphill. Even the "flat" here is uphill. This run brings you to the summit of Mt. Millicent, Mt. Tuscarora, Sunset Peak, and along Pioneer Ridge. Making for a constant climb that makes you question your life decisions.

Snowbird, Utah

Talk About Vert! This trail and I have history. To keep it short, I will never underestimate this climb again. This trail brings you to the summit of Mt. Baldy, making for an unforgettable view of Salt Lake Valley and the rest of the Wasatch Front.

Arapahoe Basin, Colorado

One word to describe Arapahoe Basin; FLOWY. The trails here are a consistent incline and decline. You cannot help but find a rhythm here. However, be aware that it is high elevation. It is humbling to realize that you cannot breath as well at 13,000 feet.
